I’ve spent the last four years at Iowa State University studying to earn my bachelor’s degree in
science majoring in computer engineering. I’m proud of my accomplishment. Though advertised
as doable in four years it’s really a five-year program. This is evident in the several summer
classes I took so I could graduate now as opposed to a year from now. I overcame several
challenges at ISU. I really enjoyed figuring out how to make my code work. This essay is going
to break down just some of the skills I learned here and how I’ve applied them.
The first big thing I learned at Iowa State that I still carry with me is the C programing language.
C is the lowest level coding language I willingly work in. I know how to write in assembly but
avoid that wherever possible. C allowed me, during CPRE 185, to write a maze navigable via a
PlayStation controller. C wasn’t the only programming language I learned at Iowa State.
I also learned JavaScript, JS for short. JS has been very useful to me. During my senior design
project, I wrote a functional on-screen keyboard using primarily JS and HTML. I did take a
course on HTML here but the summer before that I’d already taken the time to learn HTML on
my own. It’s a fun and simple language and allowed me to make this website instead of just
filling out a template as was expected of me. I really enjoy using HTML and JS and hope to use
them in the future.
The last big skill I’d like to touch on here is how to write for length. I in general try to be a man of
few words. I feel it adds more weight and I hate the idea of wasting people’s time. That said, the
mantle of collegiate writing often required I write longer than I needed to fill a length
requirement. I still don’t like spelling things out but in certain classes, I found it easy to rant
about specific topics. For example, you may notice in my general education piece the largest
paragraph is devoted to my recent world military history class. I really enjoy military history as I
find wars an easy way to check in on how a nation is doing economically and technologically. I
by no means endorse war, some of the most fascinating wars were entirely avoidable, but I
enjoy studying them long after the fighting has ended.
These are just some of the useful skills I’ve studied while attending Iowa State. I’ve learned and
improved upon my coding skills, and how to research what I don’t understand for myself. I’ve
improved my writing skills including but not limited to writing for length. I’ve sadly not found the
time to truly apply myself to say an engineering-focused club, but I was president of the Guild of
Boardgamers and Roleplayers for 2 years and I’ve taken up smaller passion projects like this
website and a web scraper I sadly never finished. I still enjoy seeing computational principles in
my everyday life despite never enjoying a Computer Science course. I’m looking forward to
graduating this August and moving on to the next chapter in my life.
